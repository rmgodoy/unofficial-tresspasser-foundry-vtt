# Tempting Fate

After you fail a check, if you know the relevant skill, you
can choose to tempt fate, making a calculated risk to
salvage your attempt. Describe what you are trying to do
and choose one of the five shadows. The Judge may
approve it or suggest another instead.

The shadow you settle on is applied to your result even on
a success. In return, you get to make the check again for
another chance to succeed.

If you tempt fate and add a shadow already chosen by the
Judge, you incur the consequences twice. For example, if
you add costly when you tempt fate and it was already
one of the warned shadows, you suffer two costly effects
instead of one.

## Tempting Fate For Allies

When an ally fails a check in your presence, you can
intervene if you know the relevant skill, tempting fate on
their behalf. You choose the shadow to add, make the
second roll, and take on all the potential consequences for
yourself.
