# Plights & Lasting States

The work of adventuring can afflict trespassers with all
manner of emotional and physical ailments; plights
represent these deleterious conditions. They are similar
to the states that can afflict characters during the combat
frame, but they usually affect their behavior during the
dungeon frame, and they last indefinitely until steps are
taken to remove them.
Plights are often gained by accidents that occur during
dungeon exploration, especially as the result of triggering
a trap or suffering a harmful or daunting shadow. Plights
can add another layer of strategy and roleplaying to
exploring the dungeon.

## Lasting States

A lasting state is a type of plight that functions like one of
the states found in Chapter III: Combat. Unlike a normal
state, it cannot be prevailed against and persists even
through multiple encounters. When a character gains a
lasting state, tell them to mark it with an asterisk* or a
small 'L' on their character sheet.
A character with a lasting state cannot gain its opposite
state, if any. For example, a character with inaccurate 2 as
a lasting state cannot gain the accurate state. The same
state can still stack with a lasting state, increasing the
INTENSITY; when the creature successfully prevails, the
returns to the lasting state INTENSITY instead of being
removed.

## Removing Plights
There are only three ways to remove a plight:
- STRIKING SUCCESS. When a character succeeds
on a task and chooses the striking spark, they
may be able to remove a plight from an ally that
views the accomplishment, if the fiction makes
sense for it.

- LIFTING SPIRITS. When the party takes the
camp travel action, a character can perform the
lift spirits activity to try to remove a plight
from one of their companions.

- WEEK'S REST. When the party receives the
benefit of a week's rest at the haven, each
character clears their plights.

## Common Plights

**Afraid**

An afraid character is hesitant and anxious. They cannot
choose to be quick or striking when they spark on a check.

**Angry**

An angry character is rash and uncooperative. They cannot
choose to be canny, quiet, or safe when they spark on a check.

**Befuddled**

A befuddled character is disoriented and confused. They
cannot add any bonus to any INTELLECT or SPIRIT checks.

**Clumsy**

A clumsy character is having trouble keeping track of their
belongings. They add costly as an extra shadow to all non-
group checks during the dungeon frame.

**Conspicuous**

A conspicuous character is coughing loudly, reeks with a
terrible odor, or otherwise draws attention. They add loud as
an extra shadow to all non-group checks they make during the
dungeon frame.

**Discouraged**

A discouraged character is expecting the worst from every
endeavor. They cannot tempt fate on skill checks.

**Distracted**

A distracted character is lost in thought or emotion, pestered
by insects, or preoccupied by something they found. They start
combat with 0 focus instead of focus equal to their SKILL
BONUS.

**Enfeebled**

An enfeebled character is too weak to help share the load.
Their inventory size is reduced by half.

**Sickly**

A sickly character is physically diminished and weak. They
cannot add any bonus to any MIGHT or AGILITY checks.

**Sluggish**

A dazed character is dazed and unresponsive. They do not roll
on initiative checks in combat, failing them automatically.
They can still roll initiative to retreat as normal
