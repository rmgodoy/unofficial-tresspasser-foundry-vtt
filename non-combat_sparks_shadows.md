# Sparks

For every five points you surpass the target number, you
add a spark to your result. Sparks are the five virtues of
the trespasser, and each spark on your success lets you
choose a benefit that colors the outcome. You cannot
apply the same spark multiple times to a single result.

## Canny

You perform the task with care and attention. You retain
something you expected to lose in the effort or avoid
paying what seemed like a necessary cost. Banishes
costly.

## Quick

You accomplish in short order something that should
have taken much longer, saving all-valuable time for
your companions. Banishes slow.

## Quiet

You slip beneath the notice of those who might oppose
your efforts. You don't draw notice from unfriendly
eyes or arouse unwanted attention from the dungeon
around you. Banishes loud.

## Safe

Even though injury might have seemed a certainty given
the task at hand, you suffer no harm doing it. Banishes
harmful.

## Striking

You display such impressive skill in your attempt that
you influence those around you. Perhaps you inspire a
watching ally or earn the favor of someone doubting
your abilities. Banishes daunting.

# Shadows

For every five points you fall short of the target number,
the Judge adds a shadow. Shadows are the looming perils
of the dungeon, and each shadow on your failure adds a
drawback that rubs salt in the wound. The Judge chooses
each penalty for you:

## Costly

You lose something of material value. Maybe the
treasure slipped out of your pack as you leapt, or your
faithful tool was finally pushed too far.

## Slow

The task took much longer than you thought, and you've
lost precious time you couldn't afford to lose. The
torches flicker. Your stomach rumbles.

## Loud

You draw attention to yourself with a loud noise or
other major disruption. If those nearby didn't know
you're here, they certainly do now.

## Harmful

You hurt yourself in some fashion. Depending on what
you tried, the harm could be anything from a few
scratches to an injury that lingers for weeks.

## Daunting

Your effort was so humiliating that those who observed
it question whether you belong here in the first place.
You might not, but you don't want them realizing that.
